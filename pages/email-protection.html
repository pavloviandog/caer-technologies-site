The Problem

Email working fine doesn't mean it's set up safely.

A domain can send and receive email without a hitch while still having gaps that leave it open to spoofing or impersonation. You'd likely never know — until a client calls asking about a suspicious message that appeared to come from you.

The good news: these issues are fixable. We find them, explain them in plain language, and take care of them for you.

⚠️

Spoofing & Impersonation

Attackers send emails appearing to come from your domain — targeting your clients, vendors, or staff.

📉

Deliverability Failures

Misconfigured authentication causes legitimate emails to land in spam or get silently rejected.

🔍

Zero Visibility

Without reporting in place, you have no way of knowing who is sending mail as your domain.

What We Check

A complete Email Authentication & Domain Health Assessment

We review every layer of your domain's email security posture — not just the obvious ones.

SPF

Sender Policy Framework

We verify which servers are authorized to send mail for your domain and clean up risky, broken, or overloaded records.

DKIM

DomainKeys Identified Mail

We validate message signing, selector health, and key strength so your outbound mail can be trusted by receiving servers.

DMARC

Domain-Based Auth & Reporting

We check policy enforcement level, alignment, and whether your domain is truly protected or just passively monitoring.

MTA-STS

Mail Transport Agent Security

We verify whether encrypted mail transport is enforced between servers and whether delivery failures are being reported.

BIMI

Brand Indicators for Messages

We assess your readiness for inbox logo display — a trust signal that requires strong DMARC enforcement to unlock.

DNS

DNS & Domain Health

We review DNSSEC, MX health, and structural issues in your domain's DNS that can affect authentication and deliverability.
