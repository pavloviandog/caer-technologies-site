Screen Sharing & Remote Meeting Problems — Fixed

⊞ Windows Mac June 22, 2025 · 5 min read By CAER Technologies

Nothing derails a meeting faster than a screen that won't share, a camera that won't turn on, or audio that cuts in and out. These problems are incredibly common — and almost always fixable in under two minutes if you know where to look. This guide covers Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Google Meet on both Windows and Mac.

Problem 1 — Screen Won't Share

This is the most common meeting complaint. The share button works, but the other participants see a black screen or get an error.

⊞ Windows — Black Screen When Sharing
  1. Right-click the meeting app icon in your taskbar and choose Run as administrator — this alone fixes it most of the time.
  2. If using a laptop with a dedicated GPU, open Settings → Display → Graphics and set your meeting app to use the integrated GPU (not the high-performance one).
  3. In Zoom: go to Settings → Share Screen → Advanced and enable Hardware Acceleration — or disable it if it's currently on. Toggle it the opposite way from what it currently is.
  4. Restart the app completely — right-click the taskbar icon and choose Quit, then reopen it.
Mac — Screen Recording Permission Not Granted
  1. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording.
  2. Make sure your meeting app (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet browser) has a checkmark. If it's there but still not working, toggle it off, wait 5 seconds, and toggle it back on.
  3. You will be prompted to quit and reopen the app — do this.
  4. If the app isn't listed at all, try sharing your screen once inside the meeting — macOS will prompt you to grant permission for the first time.

Problem 2 — Camera Not Working

⊞ Windows — Camera Blocked or Unavailable
  1. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera.
  2. Make sure Camera access is toggled on at the top.
  3. Scroll down and confirm your specific meeting app has permission enabled.
  4. Check that no other app is using the camera at the same time — Teams running in the background while joining a Zoom call is a very common culprit.
Mac — Camera Permission or Conflict
  1. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera.
  2. Confirm your meeting app is listed and enabled.
  3. If another app is using the camera (FaceTime left open, for example), quit it.
  4. Still not working? Restart your Mac — macOS occasionally holds a lock on the camera that only clears on reboot.

Problem 3 — Audio Not Working (Can't Hear or Be Heard)

🔊 Windows & Mac — Check In-App Audio Settings First
  1. Inside your meeting app, look for an audio settings or speaker/microphone icon — usually a small arrow next to the mute button.
  2. Confirm the correct microphone and speaker are selected. Meeting apps often default to a wrong device — especially after plugging in headphones or a USB adapter.
  3. Click Test Speaker and Test Mic if available.
  4. If you joined via browser (Google Meet), check that your browser has microphone and camera permission — look for the camera/lock icon in your address bar.
⊞ Windows — Microphone Access
  1. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.
  2. Confirm microphone access is on and your app has permission.
  3. Right-click the speaker icon in your taskbar → Sound settings → confirm the correct input device is set as default.
Mac — Microphone Access
  1. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.
  2. Make sure your meeting app is enabled.
  3. Go to System Settings → Sound → Input and confirm the right microphone is selected and the input volume is not at zero.

Problem 4 — Meeting App Keeps Crashing or Freezing

If the app itself becomes unstable during meetings, try these in order:

  • Update the app — outdated versions of Zoom and Teams in particular are notorious for instability. Check for updates before your next call.
  • Clear the app cache — in Zoom, go to Settings → General → Clear Cache. In Teams, sign out, close fully, and sign back in.
  • Use the browser version as a backup — meet.google.com and teams.microsoft.com both work in Chrome or Edge without installing anything.
  • Check your internet connection — video calls need at least 3–5 Mbps upload. Run a speed test at fast.com during a call issue to rule out bandwidth problems.
💡 Pro tip: Keep the browser version of your meeting platform bookmarked as a fallback. If the desktop app fails right before a call, you can join in under 30 seconds through the browser without reinstalling anything.
