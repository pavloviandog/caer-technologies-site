OneDrive / iCloud Not Syncing — How to Fix It

⊞ Windows Mac June 25, 2025 · 5 min read By CAER Technologies

That little cloud icon in your taskbar or menu bar is supposed to stay quiet. When it starts spinning forever, showing error badges, or files stop appearing on your other devices, something has broken in the sync process. The good news is that most sync problems are fixable in a few minutes without losing any data.

Before You Do Anything — Check the Service Status

Sometimes the problem isn't your computer at all. Microsoft and Apple occasionally have service outages that affect syncing. Before you start troubleshooting, check:

  • OneDrive: visit portal.office.com/servicestatus or search "Microsoft 365 service status"
  • iCloud: visit apple.com/support/systemstatus

If there's an active incident, the only fix is to wait. Come back in an hour.

Fix OneDrive Sync Issues on Windows

⊞ Step 1 — Restart OneDrive
  1. Click the OneDrive cloud icon in your system tray (bottom right, may be hidden — click the arrow to show all icons).
  2. Click the gear icon → Quit OneDrive.
  3. Press Windows + R, type %localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\OneDrive.exe and press Enter to relaunch it.
  4. Watch the icon — it should start syncing again within 30 seconds.
⊞ Step 2 — Check Storage Space
  1. Click the OneDrive icon → gear → Settings → Account.
  2. Check your storage usage. If you're at or near your limit, OneDrive stops syncing new files.
  3. Free up space by deleting old files from OneDrive, or upgrade your Microsoft 365 storage.
⊞ Step 3 — Reset OneDrive (Last Resort)
  1. Press Windows + R and type: %localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset then press Enter.
  2. The OneDrive icon will disappear for a minute — this is normal.
  3. If it doesn't reappear after 2 minutes, relaunch it using the path in Step 1 above.
  4. OneDrive will re-sync everything from scratch. Your files are safe — this does not delete anything.

Fix iCloud Drive Sync Issues on Mac

Step 1 — Pause and Resume Sync
  1. Click the iCloud icon in your menu bar.
  2. Click Pause Sync, wait 10 seconds, then click Resume Sync.
  3. Sometimes a stuck sync just needs a nudge to restart.
Step 2 — Sign Out and Back In to iCloud
  1. Go to System Settings → [Your Name] → Sign Out.
  2. Choose to keep a copy of your iCloud data on the Mac when prompted.
  3. Sign back in with your Apple ID.
  4. iCloud Drive will re-establish sync — allow a few minutes for it to complete.
Step 3 — Check iCloud Storage
  1. Go to System Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud.
  2. Check the storage bar at the top. If it's full or nearly full, syncing will stop.
  3. Click Manage to see what's using space and delete what you don't need.

Fix OneDrive Sync Issues on Mac

Using OneDrive on a Mac (common with Microsoft 365 business accounts)? The fix is slightly different.

OneDrive on Mac
  1. Click the OneDrive cloud icon in your menu bar → gear → Quit OneDrive.
  2. Reopen OneDrive from your Applications folder.
  3. If it still won't sync, go to gear → Preferences → Account → Unlink this Mac, then sign back in. This resets the sync connection without deleting your files.

Common Error Messages and What They Mean

  • "File name too long" or "Invalid characters" — OneDrive doesn't support file names over 400 characters or names containing symbols like * : ? " < > |. Rename the file.
  • "Upload blocked — sign in" — Your session expired. Click the icon and sign in again.
  • "Processing changes" that never finishes — Use the Reset OneDrive step above.
  • Files with a cloud icon that won't download — Right-click the file and choose Always keep on this device to force a local download.
💡 CAER tip: For businesses using Microsoft 365, OneDrive sync issues are often tied to licensing or tenant configuration — not the individual computer. If multiple users are having the same problem, let us take a look at your M365 setup.
