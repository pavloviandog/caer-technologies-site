Browser Running Slow or Crashing — How to Fix It

⊞ Windows Mac July 1, 2025 · 5 min read By CAER Technologies

A slow browser is one of the most common and most annoying everyday tech problems — especially when everything else on your computer seems fine. Pages take forever to load, tabs crash, the whole browser freezes up. In most cases this is fixable in under five minutes. Here's exactly what to do, in order.

Fix 1 — Close Tabs You Don't Need

Every open tab uses memory. Twenty tabs open in Chrome or Edge can consume more RAM than most other applications combined. Before doing anything else, close every tab you don't actively need right now.

💡 Better habit: Bookmark tabs you want to come back to instead of leaving them open. Your browser will thank you — and so will your computer's fans.

Fix 2 — Clear Your Cache and Cookies

Your browser stores temporary files (cache) to load sites faster, but over time this cache gets bloated and actually causes slowdowns and errors. Clearing it is safe — you won't lose saved passwords, but you will need to log back in to websites.

🌐 Chrome & Edge (Windows and Mac)
  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Delete (Windows) or Cmd + Shift + Delete (Mac).
  2. Set the time range to All time.
  3. Check Cached images and files and Cookies and other site data.
  4. Click Clear data. Restart the browser.
Safari (Mac only)
  1. In the menu bar, click Safari → Settings → Privacy.
  2. Click Manage Website Data → Remove All.
  3. Also go to Safari → Settings → Advanced and check Show Develop menu in menu bar.
  4. Then click Develop → Empty Caches from the menu bar.

Fix 3 — Disable or Remove Extensions

Browser extensions are one of the most overlooked causes of slowdowns and crashes. Some legitimate extensions are just poorly coded and consume excessive resources. Others are outright malicious — installed without your knowledge through software bundles.

🧩 How to Check and Disable Extensions
  1. Chrome: Type chrome://extensions in your address bar.
  2. Edge: Type edge://extensions in your address bar.
  3. Safari: Safari → Settings → Extensions.
  4. Disable everything except extensions you actively use and recognize. Toggle them off one at a time and test if the browser gets faster.
  5. Remove any extension you don't recognize — these are often adware.
⚠️ Watch for fake extensions. If you see extensions for "coupon finders," "PDF converters," or "search enhancers" that you didn't intentionally install, remove them immediately. These are very commonly adware that slow your browser and track your activity.

Fix 4 — Update Your Browser

Running an outdated browser version causes performance problems and security vulnerabilities. Updates are free and take less than two minutes.

🔄 How to Update
  • Chrome: Three-dot menu → Help → About Google Chrome. It checks and updates automatically.
  • Edge: Three-dot menu → Help and feedback → About Microsoft Edge.
  • Safari: Updates through System Settings → General → Software Update on Mac.
  • Firefox: Three-line menu → Help → About Firefox.

Fix 5 — Check What's Using Your Memory

If the browser is still slow after the above steps, something else on your computer may be starving it of memory.

⊞ Windows — Task Manager
  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager.
  2. Click the Memory column header to sort by usage.
  3. If your browser (Chrome, Edge) is at the top using 2–4GB or more, that's the culprit — close unused tabs.
  4. Also look for other high-memory processes. Anything unfamiliar using significant memory is worth investigating.
Mac — Activity Monitor
  1. Open Spotlight (Cmd + Space) and search for Activity Monitor.
  2. Click the Memory tab and sort by memory usage.
  3. Check if your browser or browser helper processes are at the top.
  4. Look at the Memory Pressure graph at the bottom — if it's consistently red or yellow, your Mac needs more RAM or fewer apps running.

Fix 6 — Reset or Reinstall the Browser

If none of the above helped, resetting the browser to its default state clears out corrupted settings without affecting your bookmarks and saved passwords.

🔁 Reset Chrome or Edge
  1. Chrome: Type chrome://settings/reset → Restore settings to their original defaults → Reset settings.
  2. Edge: Type edge://settings/reset → Restore settings to their default values → Reset.
  3. Your bookmarks, history, and saved passwords are preserved. Extensions are disabled and settings are cleared.

Still Slow After All of This?

If your browser is slow even after completing every step above, the browser itself may not be the problem. A slow browser on a computer that feels generally sluggish usually points to one of three things: not enough RAM, a nearly full hard drive, or malware. Check out our guide on fixing a slow computer for the next steps.
